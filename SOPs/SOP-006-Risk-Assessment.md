## 1. Purpose

Most people treat risk assessment like a compliance checkbox. 
They fill out a template once a year, submit it to whoever asked 
for it, and forget about it until next year. That approach does 
not protect the organization; it just creates a paper trail that 
gives the illusion of protection.

Real risk assessment is an ongoing conversation between what could 
go wrong, how likely it is to happen, what the impact would be, 
and what we are willing to do about it. It requires honest 
evaluation, cross-functional input, and the courage to surface 
uncomfortable findings to leadership even when the news is not good.

I built this SOP on the foundation of my graduate work in 
Cybersecurity Management and Policy combined with hands-on 
experience managing operational risk in logistics and project 
environments. The goal here is not to produce a report that 
sits in a folder. The goal is to give decision makers the 
information they need to make smart, informed choices about 
where to invest in protection and where to accept risk deliberately 
rather than accidentally.

This procedure covers both cybersecurity risk and operational risk 
because in modern organizations those two things are deeply 
connected. A supply chain disruption can have cybersecurity 
implications. A security incident can have severe operational 
consequences. Assessing them in silos produces incomplete results.
