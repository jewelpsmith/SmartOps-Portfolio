## 1. Why I Created This

Honestly, I got tired of spending hours on tasks that didn't need 
to take that long. Writing status reports, drafting meeting notes, 
organizing research — these are important but they were eating up 
time I could spend on actual strategy and problem solving.

So I built this workflow around AI tools that I actually use every 
day. Not because it's trendy, but because it genuinely changed how 
I work. This SOP documents exactly how I use AI in my PM process 
so it can be replicated, taught, and improved over time.

---

## 2. Scope

This applies to any project manager, operations lead, or logistics 
coordinator looking to integrate AI tools into their daily workflow 
without sacrificing quality, security, or accountability.

---

## 3. The Golden Rule

> AI is your assistant, not your replacement. 
> You still own every decision, every document, every outcome.
> Always review, verify, and put your own expertise into everything AI helps you create.

## 5. My Daily AI Workflow

### Morning — Planning (15 mins)
- Open Notion and review today's tasks
- Ask Claude to help prioritize based on project deadlines
- Use Zapier automations to check overnight alerts and updates
- Set intentions for the day — what needs human focus vs what AI can assist

### During the Day — Execution
- Use Claude for any writing tasks (emails, reports, documentation)
- Use NotebookLM when researching vendors, policies, or regulations
- Log all AI-assisted outputs clearly so the team knows what was AI-supported
- Never send AI-generated content without reading and personalizing it first

### End of Day — Review (10 mins)
- Summarize progress using Notion AI
- Flag anything AI got wrong or missed — this is how you get better
- Update project dashboards
- Set Zapier to send tomorrow's morning briefing automatically

## 6. How I Use AI for Specific PM Tasks

### Writing Status Reports
1. Gather raw project data (tasks completed, blockers, next steps)
2. Feed it to Claude with context: *"Write a professional status report based on this data"*
3. Read it. Edit it. Make it sound like YOU.
4. Send it.

### Running Meetings More Efficiently
1. Before the meeting — ask Claude to generate an agenda based on open tasks
2. During the meeting — take rough notes (keywords are fine)
3. After the meeting — paste notes into Claude: *"Clean this up into meeting minutes"*
4. Review, adjust, distribute to the team

### Managing Vendor Research
1. Upload vendor documents and proposals into NotebookLM
2. Ask it to compare, summarize, and flag red flags
3. Use the insights to make YOUR decision — not the AI's
4. Document the decision and reasoning for the project file

## 7. Security Considerations (CMP Framework)

This is important — AI tools are powerful but they come with 
real security responsibilities:

- **Never paste sensitive company data, PII, or confidential contracts** into public AI tools
- Use **enterprise versions** of AI tools where possible (they have stronger data protections)
- Always check your company's **Acceptable Use Policy** before using AI tools at work
- Treat AI outputs like a first draft from a junior employee — review everything
- Keep a log of where AI was used in deliverables for transparency and accountability

---

## 8. What AI Cannot Do (And Shouldn't Try To)

- Make final decisions on behalf of the team
- Replace human judgment in high-stakes situations
- Handle sensitive HR, legal, or compliance matters without human oversight
- Know your organization's internal culture, politics, or history
- Take accountability — that's always yours
