## 📊 Why Onboarding Training Matters: The Data

Before diving into the program itself, understand 
why getting onboarding right is one of the highest 
return investments an organization can make.
```
ONBOARDING BY THE NUMBERS

69%    of employees are more likely to stay with 
       a company for 3 years if they experience 
       great onboarding
       Source: SHRM

54%    of companies with a formal onboarding process 
       report higher new hire productivity
       Source: Glassdoor

$4,000 average cost to onboard a single new employee
       Source: SHRM

Up to  213% of an employee's annual salary is the 
       total cost of replacing them if they leave
       Source: SHRM

Only   12% of employees strongly agree their 
       organization does a great job onboarding
       Source: Gallup

1 in 5 new hires leave within 45 days when 
       onboarding is poor or unstructured
       Source: BambooHR
```

The conclusion is clear. Onboarding is not 
an HR formality; it is a business investment 
with measurable returns and measurable costs 
when done poorly. This program exists to make 
sure every new hire gets the same high-quality, 
structured experience regardless of who is 
managing the process on any given day.

## LEARNING OBJECTIVES

By the end of this onboarding program every 
new employee will be able to:

1. Describe the organization's mission, values, 
   and how their role contributes to operational goals
2. Access and navigate all required systems 
   and tools with confidence
3. Identify and respond to basic security threats 
   including phishing and social engineering
4. Communicate effectively within the team 
   using established tools and protocols
5. Articulate their 30-60-90 day goals and 
   how success will be measured in their role
6. Complete role-specific competencies defined 
   in their track

## MODULE 1: Welcome and Organizational Context

### 1.1 Welcome Statement

Welcome. We are genuinely glad you are here.

Starting a new job is one of the more disorienting 
experiences a professional goes through. You are 
learning new systems, new names, new processes, 
and new expectations all at the same time while 
trying to make a good first impression. That is 
a lot to manage and we want to make it as smooth 
as possible.

This program is designed to give you everything 
you need to get started with confidence. It is 
not designed to overwhelm you with information 
in the first 48 hours. Work through it at a 
reasonable pace, ask questions whenever something 
is unclear, and remember that nobody expects 
you to know everything immediately.

What we do expect is that you engage with this 
material seriously, complete each module, and 
come to your first week check-in with your 
manager ready to discuss what you have learned 
and what questions you still have.

### 1.3 How Your Role Connects to the Mission

One of the most reliable predictors of employee 
engagement is whether the employee understands 
how their work connects to something larger than 
their task list. Research from Gallup shows that 
employees who strongly agree their work is important 
are 2.5 times more likely to be engaged at work.

Before completing this module, schedule a 30-minute 
conversation with your manager to discuss:

How does this role specifically contribute to 
the organization's mission and current strategic priorities? 
What does success in this role look like at 30 days, 
90 days, and one year? What are the 2 to 3 most 
important things this role needs to get right to 
have a meaningful positive impact?

Document the answers. They will anchor your 
30-60-90 day plan in Module 6.

### 2.2 Tool Orientation Guide

**Notion: Your Operational Hub**

Notion is where projects live, documentation 
is stored, and team collaboration happens. 
Think of it as the organization's brain; 
if something important happened or was decided, 
it should be findable in Notion.
```
FIRST STEPS IN NOTION

Day 1:  Complete your team profile page
        Explore the team workspace structure
        Locate your onboarding checklist database entry
        Find and bookmark your department's home page

Day 2:  Review active project boards relevant to your role
        Understand how tasks are assigned and tracked
        Learn how to update task status and add notes
        Find the SOP library and locate SOPs for your role

Week 1: Attend Notion orientation with your manager
        Complete your first task update in the system
        Ask questions about anything that is unclear
```

**Jira: Project and Issue Tracking**

Jira is used for structured project management 
and issue tracking. If your role involves working 
on projects with defined sprints or release cycles, 
Jira is where that work is planned and tracked.
```
JIRA BASICS FOR NEW HIRES

Boards:     Visual view of work in progress; 
            shows what is To Do, In Progress, and Done
Issues:     Individual tasks, bugs, or work items 
            assigned to team members
Sprints:    Time-boxed work cycles; typically 
            1 to 2 weeks for Agile teams
Epics:      Large bodies of work broken into 
            smaller issues
Velocity:   How much work the team completes 
            per sprint; used for planning
```

**Slack: Team Communication**

Slack is the primary real-time communication 
tool. The general rule for channel usage is 
keep project conversations in project channels, 
keep team announcements in announcement channels, 
and use direct messages for private conversations 
not relevant to the broader team.
```
SLACK ETIQUETTE

Response time expectations:
  Direct messages:    Within 2 to 4 business hours
  Channel mentions:   Within 4 business hours
  General channels:   Review daily; respond when relevant

Status updates:
  Set your status when in meetings, on leave, or unavailable
  Use Do Not Disturb outside of working hours

File sharing:
  Share files via links to Notion or Drive; 
  not as Slack attachments which are harder to find later
```

### 3.1 Why Security Is Part of Your Onboarding
```
THE NUMBERS THAT EXPLAIN WHY

74%    of all data breaches involve a human element
       Source: Verizon DBIR 2023

$4.45M average cost of a data breach in 2023
       Source: IBM Cost of a Data Breach Report 2023

300    days is the average time to identify and 
       contain a data breach
       Source: IBM 2023

83%    of organizations experienced more than 
       one data breach in 2022 to 2023
       Source: IBM 2023

91%    of cyberattacks begin with a phishing email
       Source: Deloitte
```

Security is part of your onboarding because 
you are part of the organization's security 
posture from Day 1. Not after you complete 
a compliance training. Not after your 90-day 
review. From the moment you have access to 
organizational systems you are both protected 
by and responsible for the organization's security.

### 3.2 Your Security Responsibilities
```
EVERY EMPLOYEE IS RESPONSIBLE FOR:

Protecting credentials
  Never sharing passwords with anyone including IT staff
  Using MFA on every account that supports it
  Using the organizational password manager
  Changing passwords immediately if compromise is suspected

Recognizing threats
  Identifying phishing emails before clicking
  Reporting suspicious communications to IT Security
  Verifying unusual requests through separate channels
  Never plugging in unknown USB drives or devices

Handling data responsibly
  Following the data classification policy
  Never sharing confidential data via personal email
  Locking your screen when stepping away
  Using the VPN when working outside the office

Reporting incidents
  Reporting suspicious activity immediately; not tomorrow
  Not attempting to investigate or fix security issues alone
  Preserving evidence by not deleting suspicious emails
  Knowing who to contact: [IT Security contact]
```

### 3.3 Security Setup Checklist
```
COMPLETE BEFORE END OF WEEK 1

☐ MFA enabled on all organizational accounts
☐ Password manager configured with unique 
  strong passwords for all accounts
☐ VPN tested and working from home network
☐ IT Security contact saved in phone and email
☐ Phishing reporting procedure understood
☐ Data classification policy reviewed
☐ Acceptable Use Policy signed and submitted to HR
☐ Security awareness training scheduled 
  (full program in CYBER-001)
```

## MODULE 6: 30-60-90 Day Success Framework

The 30-60-90 day framework is the single most 
effective tool for helping new employees transition 
from learning to contributing to leading in their role. 
Research from Harvard Business Review shows that 
new employees who have a structured 90-day plan 
are significantly more likely to meet performance 
expectations in their first year.
```
30-60-90 DAY FRAMEWORK

DAYS 1 TO 30: LEARN
Primary Focus: Understand before acting

Goals for this phase:
  Complete all onboarding modules and competency checklists
  Build relationships with immediate team and key stakeholders
  Develop deep understanding of current processes and systems
  Identify 3 to 5 areas where you have questions or see opportunities
  Complete first role-specific deliverable with manager guidance

Success Indicators:
  Can operate all required systems independently
  Knows who to contact for what without asking
  Has completed all compliance and security requirements
  Manager confirms foundational competency in role

────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DAYS 31 TO 60: CONTRIBUTE
Primary Focus: Add value within existing frameworks

Goals for this phase:
  Own a defined scope of work independently
  Contribute meaningfully to team meetings and discussions
  Complete first significant deliverable without close supervision
  Identify at least one process improvement opportunity
  Build relationships beyond immediate team

Success Indicators:
  Delivering work on time and to expected quality standard
  Proactively communicating blockers before they cause delays
  Receiving positive feedback from manager and peers
  Contributing ideas in team settings

────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DAYS 61 TO 90: LEAD
Primary Focus: Drive outcomes and begin to shape your area

Goals for this phase:
  Leading at least one project or process area independently
  Proposing and beginning to implement at least one improvement
  Mentoring or supporting newer or less experienced team members
  Establishing yourself as a reliable resource in your domain
  Completing 90-day review with clear goals for next quarter

Success Indicators:
  Trusted to work independently on significant work
  Others coming to you for guidance in your area
  Manager describing you as fully ramped and contributing
  Clear goals established for next 90 days
```
