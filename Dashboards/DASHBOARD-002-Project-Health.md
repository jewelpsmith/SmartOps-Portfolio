## 💡 Why This Dashboard Exists

Managing one project is straightforward. Managing five 
projects simultaneously while keeping every stakeholder 
informed, every deadline visible, and every risk on 
the radar is a different challenge entirely.

This dashboard was built for exactly that situation. 
It gives project managers and leadership a single view 
across all active projects so that nothing slips through 
the cracks because it was buried in a project board 
that nobody checked this week.

The health indicators here are deliberately simple. 
Green, Amber, Red. Every stakeholder from a junior 
coordinator to a C-suite executive can read this 
dashboard in 30 seconds and know exactly where things 
stand. That is the point.

## SECTION 1: Portfolio Summary

One view. Everything you need to know before diving deeper.
```
ACTIVE PORTFOLIO SNAPSHOT
Week Ending: [Date]
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Total Active Projects:          __
Projects On Track (Green):      __
Projects At Risk (Amber):       __
Projects In Trouble (Red):      __
Projects Completed This Month:  __
New Projects Initiated:         __

Overall Portfolio Health:    🟢 HEALTHY / 🟡 AT RISK / 🔴 CRITICAL

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

**Health Definition Key**

🟢 HEALTHY
Schedule variance within 5%; budget variance within 5%; 
no unresolved critical risks; team capacity within normal range.

🟡 AT RISK
Schedule or budget variance between 5% and 15%; at least 
one high-rated risk open without a mitigation plan; 
or resource constraints impacting delivery timeline.

🔴 CRITICAL
Schedule or budget variance exceeding 15%; critical risk 
with no mitigation plan in place; scope significantly 
impacted; or stakeholder escalation received.

instant and stakeholders never have to relearn the layout.

## SECTION 3: Milestone Tracker

Milestones are the checkpoints that tell you whether 
a project is actually on track or just busy. A team 
that completes tasks but misses milestones is working 
hard in the wrong direction.
```
MILESTONE TRACKER — ALL ACTIVE PROJECTS
Week Ending: [Date]
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PROJECT     MILESTONE              DUE DATE    STATUS      OWNER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PRJ-001     [Milestone Name]       [Date]      ✅ Complete  [Name]
PRJ-001     [Milestone Name]       [Date]      🔄 On Track  [Name]
PRJ-001     [Milestone Name]       [Date]      ⚠️ At Risk   [Name]
PRJ-002     [Milestone Name]       [Date]      ✅ Complete  [Name]
PRJ-002     [Milestone Name]       [Date]      🔴 Overdue   [Name]
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

STATUS KEY
✅ Complete:  Milestone achieved on or before due date
🔄 On Track:  Milestone in progress; no risk to due date
⚠️ At Risk:   Milestone may be missed; mitigation needed
🔴 Overdue:   Milestone passed due date without completion
```

Any milestone marked At Risk or Overdue requires a 
written action plan from the Project Manager within 
24 hours of the dashboard being published.

## SECTION 4: Resource Allocation View

Resource conflicts are one of the top three reasons 
projects fall behind. This view makes it visible 
before it becomes a problem rather than after.
```
RESOURCE ALLOCATION SUMMARY
Week of: [Date]
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

TEAM MEMBER         PROJECTS ASSIGNED    ALLOCATION    STATUS
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
[Name]              PRJ-001, PRJ-003     85%           ⚠️ High
[Name]              PRJ-002              60%           ✅ Healthy
[Name]              PRJ-001, PRJ-002,    110%          🔴 Over
                    PRJ-004
[Name]              PRJ-003              45%           ✅ Healthy

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ALLOCATION STATUS KEY
✅ Healthy:    50% to 85% allocated; optimal range
⚠️ High:       86% to 99% allocated; monitor closely
🔴 Over:       100%+ allocated; immediate rebalancing needed
⚪ Low:        Below 50%; consider additional assignments
```

Any team member showing Over allocation must be 
addressed by the Operations Manager within 48 hours. 
Sustained overallocation is both a performance risk 
and a burnout risk that no dashboard metric can fix 
after the fact.

## 🎨 Visualization and Presentation Options

**For weekly team visibility**
Build as a Notion database with board view showing 
health card for each project. Team members update 
their sections directly; PM reviews and publishes Friday.

**For stakeholder reporting**
Use Gamma to generate a clean visual version from 
the compiled data. Takes under 10 minutes and 
produces a shareable link that updates automatically.

**For executive briefings**
Build in PowerPoint using the organization's branded 
template. One slide per project health card; milestone 
tracker on a single summary slide; resource view 
included only if there are active conflicts to surface.

**For archived records**
Maintain historical snapshots in Excel with one 
tab per reporting week. This creates a visual 
timeline of each project's health over its lifetime; 
invaluable for post-project retrospectives and 
for building estimates on future similar projects.

---

## 🔐 Security and Data Handling

Project health data contains budget figures, resource 
names, and strategic project information classified 
as internal and confidential.

Dashboard distribution limited to authorized stakeholders 
only; Notion database access managed via workspace 
permissions; exported reports shared via password 
protected links for projects involving sensitive 
financial or strategic data; resource allocation 
view distributed to leadership only; and historical 
Excel archive stored in secured shared drive with 
access restricted to Project Management Office.
