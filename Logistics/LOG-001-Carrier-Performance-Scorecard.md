## 💡 Why This Scorecard Exists

Most organizations evaluate carriers the same 
way they evaluate every vendor relationship: 
informally, reactively, and only when something 
goes wrong badly enough that someone complains 
loudly enough for leadership to notice.

By that point the damage is already done. A 
customer has been let down, an operations team 
has spent days cleaning up an exception that 
should never have happened, and the conversation 
about carrier performance is happening under 
pressure rather than from a position of data 
and clarity.

This scorecard was built to change that dynamic. 
It creates a structured, consistent, data-driven 
framework for evaluating every carrier in the 
network on the metrics that actually matter to 
operations; not just price, which is what most 
carrier conversations default to.

The philosophy behind it is simple: carriers 
who perform well deserve to grow their share 
of our business; carriers who underperform 
get a clear, documented opportunity to improve 
or they get replaced. No ambiguity, no 
relationship-based exceptions, no surprises 
on either side.

## CATEGORY 1: Delivery Performance
### Maximum Score: 35 Points

Delivery performance is the most heavily weighted 
category because it is the most directly visible 
to customers and stakeholders. A carrier can 
communicate beautifully and document everything 
perfectly; but if shipments consistently arrive 
late, none of that matters.

---

### 1.1 On-Time Delivery Rate
**Maximum: 20 Points**

On-time delivery is measured against the original 
committed delivery date at the time of booking; 
not against revised dates issued after a delay 
has already occurred. Rescheduling a late shipment 
and then delivering on the new date does not 
count as on-time.
```
SCORING SCALE

≥ 98% on-time:      20 points   🟢 Exceptional
95% to 97.9%:       16 points   🟢 Meets Target
92% to 94.9%:       12 points   🟡 Below Target
89% to 91.9%:        8 points   🟡 At Risk
85% to 88.9%:        4 points   🔴 Underperforming
Below 85%:           0 points   🔴 Critical; formal review required

This Quarter's Rate:     __%
Points Awarded:          __ / 20
```

---

### 1.2 Transit Time Accuracy
**Maximum: 10 Points**

Transit time accuracy measures whether the 
carrier's quoted transit times reflect reality. 
A carrier that consistently quotes 2-day transit 
and delivers in 3 days is creating planning 
problems for the operations team regardless 
of whether the technical delivery date was met.
```
SCORING SCALE

Actual transit within 0 to 4 hours of quoted:    10 points
Actual transit within 4 to 8 hours of quoted:     8 points
Actual transit within 8 to 24 hours of quoted:    5 points
Actual transit 1 to 2 days beyond quoted:         2 points
Actual transit more than 2 days beyond quoted:    0 points

This Quarter's Average Variance:     __ hours
Points Awarded:                      __ / 10
```

---

### 1.3 First Attempt Delivery Success Rate
**Maximum: 5 Points**

Failed first delivery attempts create downstream 
costs including re-delivery fees, customer 
dissatisfaction, and operational coordination 
time. This metric holds carriers accountable 
for preparation and communication before delivery.
```
SCORING SCALE

≥ 97% first attempt success:    5 points
94% to 96.9%:                   4 points
90% to 93.9%:                   3 points
85% to 89.9%:                   1 point
Below 85%:                      0 points

This Quarter's Rate:     __%
Points Awarded:          __ / 5
```

## CATEGORY 2: Exception Management
### Maximum Score: 25 Points

How a carrier handles exceptions tells you more 
about them than how they handle normal shipments. 
Anyone can deliver a straightforward shipment 
on time. The carriers worth keeping in your 
network are the ones who own their exceptions, 
communicate proactively, and resolve problems 
without requiring the operations team to chase them.

---

**Maximum: 10 Points**

```
2% to 3%:                     6 points   🟡 Below Target
3% to 5%:                     3 points   🔴 At Risk
Above 5%:                     0 points   🔴 Critical

This Quarter's Exception Rate:   __%
Points Awarded:                  __ / 10
```

### 2.2 Proactive Exception Notification
**Maximum: 8 Points**

Proactive notification means the carrier contacts 
us about a delay or exception before we have 
to contact them. This distinction matters enormously 
in operations because proactive communication 
allows us to manage customer and stakeholder 
expectations in advance rather than explaining 
a problem after it has already caused impact.
```
SCORING SCALE

Proactive notification on ≥ 95% of exceptions:    8 points
Proactive notification on 85% to 94%:             6 points
Proactive notification on 70% to 84%:             4 points
Proactive notification on 50% to 69%:             2 points
Proactive notification on below 50%:              0 points

This Quarter's Rate:     __%
Points Awarded:          __ / 8
```

## CATEGORY 3: Communication and Service
### Maximum Score: 20 Points

Communication quality is one of the most 
subjective but most operationally important 
dimensions of carrier performance. Poor communication 
from a carrier forces the operations team to 
spend time chasing information that should 
have been provided proactively; time that 
could be spent on higher value work.

---

### Maximum Score: 10 Points

Documentation errors create ripple effects 
across operations; customs delays, billing 
disputes, compliance issues, and audit findings 
all trace back to inaccurate shipping documentation. 
This category holds carriers accountable for 
the quality of their paperwork; not just their 
delivery performance.

## CATEGORY 5: Security and Compliance
### Maximum Score: 10 Points

Supply chain security is not a theoretical concern. 
Cargo theft, data breaches involving shipment 
manifests, and supply chain infiltration by 
bad actors are documented and growing threats 
in the logistics industry. This category evaluates 
whether carriers take security seriously as 
an operational responsibility.
```
SECURITY AND COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST

C-TPAT certification current:                    ☐ Yes (2 pts)  ☐ No
Data handling agreement signed and current:       ☐ Yes (2 pts)  ☐ No
No security incidents in past 12 months:          ☐ Yes (2 pts)  ☐ No
Driver and staff background check program:        ☐ Yes (2 pts)  ☐ No
Secure chain of custody procedures documented:    ☐ Yes (2 pts)  ☐ No

Points Awarded:    __ / 10

SECURITY FLAGS (automatic score reduction)
Any active security incident involving our shipments:  -5 points
Expired data handling agreement:                       -3 points
Undisclosed security breach discovered:                -10 points
```
