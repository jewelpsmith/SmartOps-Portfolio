## 💡 The Problem This Solves

Before this automation existed, onboarding a new employee meant 
someone in HR or Operations manually sending welcome emails, 
creating Notion pages, notifying the IT team, updating the 
employee roster in Google Sheets, and messaging the team on 
Slack. Every single time. For every single hire.

That is anywhere from 45 minutes to 2 hours of manual work 
per new hire, spread across multiple people, with plenty of 
room for things to be forgotten or done out of order.

This workflow eliminates all of that. One trigger, and the 
entire onboarding sequence fires automatically, consistently, 
and in the right order every single time.
